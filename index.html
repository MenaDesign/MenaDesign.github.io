About Me

Hi, I am Mena Ye, a web designer, illustrator, and developer who is everyone's vitamin C =)

Growing up as a piano player and going to an art school at an early age has gifted me with the ability to solve problems innovatively and create lots of beautiful things.

I began my passion for web design and development during my pursuit for a computer science degree at U of T. This passion was fostered during my high school years, where I experimented with developing static websites for fun.

Work Process

Here are my usual stages for developing a website:

  1. Analyze the project
  2. Plan, sketch, and design
  3. Implement
  4. Test, tweak, and optimize
  5. Verify and Maintain

This model is commonly known as the waterfall model and is one of the many great methods I like to use for optimized website development. I usually like to incorporate the waterfall model into the following process:

  1. Research user requirements
  2. Create design
  3. Get feedback from the user
  4. Adapt
  5. Repeat until requirements are met
  6. Develop the website

This process is commonly referred to as the user-centered design process (UCD).

I am also familiar with other development processes such as the Agile software development. In the end, it's all about finding the right tools to best meet the requirements of the user.
